# Rule: never fabricate numbers

A fabricated precise metric is the fastest way to destroy trust. This is the single
most important rule in the skill.

- A **landing rate**, **fee percentile**, or **"X% → Y%" projection** must come from
  on-chain / client data (`skill/measuring-reliability.md`) **or** be explicitly
  labelled a *modelled estimate with its assumption stated*.
- A **Reliability Score** must be **derived from the rubric** in
  `skill/reliability-score.md` and shown with its per-control breakdown — never a
  vibe, never a round number pulled from the air.
- A **"projected score"** equals the **sum of recovered control weights**, not a
  predicted landing percentage. Keep the two ideas separate: the *score* grades the
  setup; the *landing rate* grades the outcome.
- If a value cannot be measured or inspected, say so (`unknown`) and exclude it from
  any claim. Do not guess it into a number.
